The Plateau Auberge Serial Murders

“I received a letter from 17 years ago.” Ron continued, “Take a look. This was in my mailbox.” The postmark was indeed from 17 years ago, and the writing was faded. The letter was addressed to the manager of Maison du Kamonohashi.

“It’s an invitation to dinner at an auberge. They sent along the menu as a greeting.”

The menu was filled with super fancy food, and the base is French Cuisine.

Ron responded, “My maternal grandmother owned the property and my grandfather was the manager. When he died, my mother managed it but gave me the keys after I got expelled from BLUE.”

“Hmm, really? My mom is a bit of an oddball, she’s currently making 50 trips around the world.”

“Of course not.”

“As far as I know, she’s not interested in that stuff. We have things in common but also a lot of differences. For example, she never gets angry.”

Toto thought back to Ron’s temper.

“He died of an illness before I was born, but don’t worry about it. I was raised by my mom, so I can’t even imagine having a father. More importantly, let’s get ready, Toto! The date on the invitation is today! We’ve gotta go, right?”

“This was also in the envelope.” He showed a beautifully cut emerald.
“We have to return it, don’t we? It looks quite valuable. We’re going on an adventure to solve the mystery of this letter!” He started humming his strange Kamoo, Kamoo, Kamoo song.


- - - - - - - - - - - -

They went on a gondola lift that stretched across a forest.

“An auberge is a restaurant with an inn attached, pretty much a restaurant where you can stay the night.” Another person interrupted. “I’m staying there for work, but I’m a little nervous since it’s my first time, so I’m glad to have company.”

“Your luggage is pretty light, are you from the area?” Ron inquired.

“I came from America, but I’m based in a Tokyo hotel.”

“I’m a lawyer who travels all around the world, let me know if you’re ever in trouble.” He handed them a business card. Tiger Dan - Lawyer.
Ron handed him something of his own in return, “Here’s my brown sugar business card!”

Tiger laughed, “I’m glad to have been able to have proper conversation with you, unlike with that English gardener over there. All I got from 30 minutes of trying to talk to him is that he brought those flowers from his own garden.”


- - - - - - - - - - - -

“Welcome! I’m Sakai, the chef! I’m sorry this is so sudden but we’re short staffed right now so I’m running things alone. Apologies for being unable to give a warmer welcome!”

Ron asked, “Are you the one who sent this invitation?”

“I’m just a hired chef so I have nothing to do with the invitations. Here’s the key to your room.”

Ron continued, “Who’s the owner?”

“I’ve never met them… a-anyways, please come to the dining room at 6 o’clock! See you later!”

“Ciao! What mystery? I’m Mia Costa, a high school student from Italy!”

Ron greeted her as well, and asked “Were you invited too?”

“Yes, by my aunt who lives in Japan.”

“Thanks to my aunt's influence, I love Japanese culture, and I’ve studied the language on my own! Anyways, what’s the mystery?”

“The invitation was sent 17 years ago.” Ron responded, “And there was an emerald.”

“This heirloom from my mother is also an emerald!” She lifted her necklace.

Ron smirked, “What a strange coincidence. But I guess that's the perfect way to start off the adventure to solve this mystery.”


- - - - - - - - - - - -


In the evening, all of the guests gathered around a table. A taxi driver named Tom Sasaoka, security company executive Karen Lily, surgeon Bill Clark, and… an odd teddy bear.

Ron quickly caught onto the guests’ backgrounds, impressing them, and starting conversation.

The first meal was an edible flower salad – that Ron covered in a thick coating of brown sugar syrup.

The second meal – lobster soup.

As the meals were served, Ron started to notice how the guests were getting unsettled, as if recalling something.

Third meal: fresh fish steamed with white wine and served with a foam garnish.

The fourth meal is three kinds of roasted wild game.

Dr. Clark stood up quickly after eating, “Sorry, but I’m exhausted… I’ll head back to my room and rest. The food was great!”

“That’s too bad…” Sakai the chef sighed, “There’s cake made with chestnut mousse.”

All of the guests suddenly started making excuses to not eat, when suddenly, the gardener spoke up.

“If only I hadn’t driven my truck that day… I shouldn’t have come to this place!” said the gardener, who was named Lenny Gardner.

Ron’s mind was already working to figure out whatever was beneath the surface of the tension.


“The food was crazy delicious, but everyone barely ate. Only Mia wasn’t acting strange. It’s like they were disturbed every time the food came out.”

Ron chuckled, “There’s my partner’s reasoning. At this rate, you’ll be a master detective a hundred years from now!~”

- - - - - - - - - - - -

Shouts rang through the hallway.

“Gardner! Gardner!”

“What’s the matter?!”

“I heard a weird noise from my room beside Gardner’s.” Tiger stated.

Lily added, “I also heard a strange sound, so I figured we’d check up on him before breakfast… Last night’s menu is taped to the door, and he’s not responding.”

Sakai continued, “We tried to open the door, but I don’t have the key…”

“In that case, I can-” As Ron suggested to help, Lily cut him off.

“I can open it, I can handle a lock like this.”

“Hmph.” Ron grumbled.

However, upon opening the door… the gardener was lying in a bed of flowers, with an ice pick stabbing his chest.



“Gardner!”


“I can’t believe this!” Dr. Clark exclaimed.

Dr. Clark continued, “That’s a shock! I heard something about an apartment manager during dinner, but…”
Ron responded, “That’s me. Officer Isshiki is the metropolitan police’s most outstanding detective.”

Sakai was instructed to contact the local police, and Dr. Clark was asked to assist. Ron pretended to be Isshiki’s crime scene assistant as he lay down beside the corpse.

The doctor claimed gardner died about 3-4 hours ago, and that he died in pain. Ron looked out the window, and quickly called over Toto, “Officer Isshiki, look!”

Ron argued, “That’s impossible. The window is locked.”

But so was the door to the room, meaning this was a locked room murder.

While Toto thought hard to make sense of the situation, Ron stated, “The flowers scattered around the corpse and the menu are the bigger mysteries.”

And then… an emerald. An identical one to the emerald Ron received.

“I can’t get a call through!” Sakai shouted. “Not with my phone or the landline!”

Everyone had no signal, and Sakai confirmed the only route reaching the auberge was the gondola ride. The mountain road was closed off due to a landslide, meaning…

They quickly rushed to the gondola ride, and sure enough, it was closed off, with an “Out of Service” sign that Sakai did not anticipate.

Before dawn, everyone was in their rooms, meaning nobody has an alibi. Suddenly, Sasaoka ran off.

“Here, Toto. There’s nowhere left to run, Sasaoka. Why’d you run in the first place?”

“Isn’t it obvious?! Because you’re here!”



Ron frowned, “Me?”

“The dinner menu, the murder, the emeralds, it’s all a plot to blackmail me!”



“What do you mean?”



“I haven’t forgotten about you!”

Ron sighed, “I’m sorry but I don’t know you. I’m not your enemy, so please tell us more about this instead.”

Sasaoka hesitantly agreed. “Promise you won’t turn me in.”

“Depends on the situation, I might look the other way if it was out of your hands.” Ron replied before Toto could contest.

Sasaoka started.
“I realized it as soon as the first dish came out last night. It was the same menu as when a man I’d met online treated me to a meal in London 17 years ago. Back then, I worked as a smuggler in addition to driving a taxi. The man from the restaurant came to me with a request, he said there was a man with a pregnant woman looking for a taxi, so he asked that I pick them up and drop them off at a certain hospital. A few days later… the body of the man I picked up was found dead in the Thames. Ever since then, I’ve tried to convince myself that the ride had nothing to do with what happened, but now it’s coming back to me…”


Ron commented, “So you were used by the man from the restaurant.”

“He was a conniving, cowardly, cruel bastard! And he looked exactly like you!” Sasaoka pointed at Ron. “His height, build, even his eyes were just like yours! Like how you pour brown sugar on your food, he poured maple syrup on his!”



“But I was just a kid!”



“That makes sense, but the resemblance was so strong! It’s also true that you don’t have a crescent birthmark on your cheek… The more I look at you, the more you look alike… are you that guy’s son or something?!”

A grim expression crossed Ron’s face, and before Toto could register, Ron was gone.
Toto followed Ron back, and upon finding him, he asked, “Hey, what’s wrong?”

“I’ve… seen a picture of my parents together, and my father had a crescent birth mark on his cheek.”



A flashback came to Ron’s mind.



“Max and Molly, playing dinosaurs is boring now that we’re three…”

A man laughed, “You’re a riot! You’ll be a big deal someday, Ron! Even more than me, your dad!”

Before Ron could properly process the memory, a scream broke through.

Another murder was about to be discovered.



- - - - - - - - - - - -



Last night’s menu was taped onto a door again.

Sasaoka was in the bath with a knife in his chest, and the water dyed blood red.

According to Dr. Clark, Sasaoka hadn’t been dead for long, and the cause of death was the knife, and according to Ron, since Sasaoka’s hair was wet and the water was splashed on all four walls, it seems like he was attacked while bathing and struggled with the killer. They found some red food coloring too that seemed to have been used to color the water because Sasaoka’s knife was intact in him, and the color of the water was far too bloody to have been caused by Sasaoka’s wound alone.

The flowers and the food coloring managed to get an idea in Ron’s head.



- - - - - - - - - - - -



They gathered round the dining table, and Toto explained the situation.

Dr. Clark, Tiger, and Mia were all in their rooms, while Lily was in the garden, and Sakai was in the kitchen. Nobody had an alibi, meaning they were all suspects.
Lily started shouting, “You’re going on about that again? I’m tired of this! We’re getting killed one by one and you still suspect us! Can’t you exclude me? I wasn’t acquainted with the victim, and I didn’t even know his name! We weren’t connected in the slightest!-”

Ron cut her off, “Are you sure about that?”

Think back to the previous events and details. So far, what do the two victims have in common?

Open ended question.

Answer: Their deaths were modeled after the dinner course they all shared.

Toto continued, “Actually, there was a pattern between the 2 murders. Both of them were modeled after the courses on the menu we had last night.”

Sakai quickly responded, “I don’t know anything about this! I just made the food like I was told! I’m just an employee!”

“Perhaps Sakai didn’t know, but Sasaoka did. He said the meal was the exact same one he ate in London 17 years ago with a man who had a crescent birthmark on his cheek who was going to hire him for a job. All of you know him too, right?” Toto and Ron continued.

“All of you know him too, right? There’s no point in playing dumb, aside from Mia, all of you were pretty spooked by the food.” Ron commented. “It was no coincidence that brought all of us here. The food and all of us were specially chosen. There are three more dishes left, forget about the man with the crescent birthmark, if you don’t want to die, start talking. We won’t solve this unless we figure out the connection.” Ron warned.

Tiger started, “I also took a job from that man after meeting him online 17 years ago. He treated me to the same meal. For the job, he wanted me to bring a certain research outside from an academic conference. He said there’d be a pregnant woman nearby, so I just had to casually lead him there. He paid me with an emerald, I sold it for cash though, so i don’t have it anymore.”

Ron added, “The researcher was found dead in the Thames a few days later.”

“Hey, I had nothing to do with his death!” Tiger responded.

“Sasaoka said he dropped the researcher and pregnant woman off at a hospital, you were at the hospital, weren’t you?”

“I met that man 17 years ago too, he treated me to the same dinner, and offered me a job in exchange for an emerald. After examining the woman, I gave the man an anesthesia shot by telling him it was a nutrient supplement, laid him down in the flower bed behind the hospital, and that was the end of my job.”

Ron corrected, “Not just those three. Gardner yelled ‘If only I hadn’t driven my truck that day!’, and Lily showed us her lock picking skills. Those hands probably came in handy.”

Tiger interrupted, “Wait, I lost my wallet last night! You took it, didn’t you?!”

“Fine, here!” She handed it to him before continuing, “17 years ago in London, like everyone else, I took a job from that man! I was instructed to lock the door of a warehouse. Thinking back, a researcher who was unconscious was brought there from the hospital.

The full picture started to show.

“The targeted researcher was at a conference, Tiger Dan got close to him, brought him outside, then they ran into the pregnant woman who was in bad shape and the researcher tried to help her. Then they headed to a hospital in a taxi driven by Sasaoka. Dr. Clark gave the researcher anesthesia, and laid him in a flowerbed, Gardner loaded him into a truck to bring him to the warehouse, the one Lily used her lock picking skills on. Finally, the man with the crescent birthmark appeared and murdered the researcher before dumping his body in the Thames.” Ron deduced.

“Sakai does seem like he was just chosen to be the chef for this, but Mia was there.”

“She was the baby carried by the pregnant woman.”

Mia remarked, “It is true that I’m 17 years old, and I have an emerald keepsake, and my mom said she made a mistake when she was pregnant with me… I can’t believe she got involved in a crime though…”

Clark responded, “She was probably just as clueless as the rest of us. Nobody could’ve known that Dr. Jim Gore would be killed-”

Ron exclaimed, “This case was the Jim Gore murder case?!”

“At BLUE, this case is one of the few I know of that’s regarded as the work of the M family.”



- - - - - - - - - - - -

Back in England, Milo grinned to himself.

“To think, this day has finally come after 17 years… dinner is just getting started, Ron Kamonohashi.”

“If all of us are connected to Dr. Gore’s murder… What's Ron’s connection?” Dr. Clark asked.

Lily repeated, “I’ve been thinking this the whole time but you look just like that man!”

“Now that you mention it, that man poured maple syrup on his food like Kamonohashi does with brown sugar.”

“What are you to that vile murderer?!” Lily exclaimed, and the others followed in pointing fingers at Ron.

“Are you doing the same thing as that man?! Are you the one behind the murders?!”

“How are we supposed to believe that?!”

“It could be all made up?!”

Tiger interrupted, “In Kamonohashi’s defense, I’d like to remind you that everyone’s story would be just as suspect.”

Mia asked, “Are we sure we’re the only ones in this auberge?”

Lily commented, “The murderer could be hiding somewhere!”

Sakai replied, “Yes, but the doors are all locked.”

“I’ll pick the locks. Let’s look.” Lily said.

But then the other guests commented, saying that they were getting exhausted, so only Lily, Ron, Toto, and Mia went.

They checked all of the rooms, but nothing was off. Mia and Lily decided to go back to their own rooms, and Ron and Toto were left alone.

“Thanks for your help earlier, Toto.”

“How can I not? The man has too many common traits with my father, and I’m starting to get the feeling that my father was still alive 17 years ago. I told you I was missing memories from my childhood, but earlier, I got a clear glimpse of a memory of him. My father was a cunning criminal who got others wrapped up in a murder, I’m a cunning hypocrite who had inconvenient memories erased, and on top of that, the M family is connected to Dr. Gore’s murder- wait. There’s another door here.”

“I’m good at picking locks too, remember? But this door isn’t even locked… hmm, it’s a storage room.”

The room looked like a storage room for party and holiday decoration – but a familiar emerald stood out, on top of a picture frame…

As Ron lifted it, the face of the father he’d seen in his memories was on the image – the man with the crescent birth mark.



“Father…?”

“I don’t wanna pick locks anymore, daddy!”

“That’s my boy! The son of Eliot Moriarty!” I didn’t expect you to unlock 100 different locks this fast! Great job, buddy!”

“Father… just called himself Eliot Moriarty…’
