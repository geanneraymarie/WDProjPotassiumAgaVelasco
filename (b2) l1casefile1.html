THE CASE OF THE METROPOLITAN SERIAL DROWNINGS

"There's only one person that can solve this case," Toto's senior, Kiku, told him.
And that's how Toto found himself at the door of Maison du Kamonohashi. He made his way up to the unit Kiku directed him to.
Kiku had directed him to a private detective who was... rather unconventional. The detective was apparently also the manager of the building, which made him think perhaps he wasn't very successful in solving cases.
Still, Kiku-san had endorsed him. Ron Kamonohashi.
And then again... they were desperate.
As he reached the unit, before he could even ring the doorbell, a voice resounded:
Ron : "Don't get your hopes up. Do it yourself!"
"Huh?!" Toto stepped back in surprise.
"Go back and pray at the shrine again!"

The door creaked open, revealing a messy, unkempt man.
...this was the detective Kiku-san recommended?

"Does saying your thoughts out loud satisfy you? Well, anyways, do you want to come in?" Ron asked.
Still, Toto glanced back and forth between Ron and the picture before snapping back to his senses.

As he stepped inside, he was shocked once again upon seeing Ron simply collapse to the floor, before realizing that the entire unit was covered in... cushions?!
"This... is the floor of sloth. It knows my daily boredom and despair."
Toto glanced around the room. The unit was dull, since the blinds were completely shut. Ron also mentioned that he didn't have access to a TV or the internet.
"I'm completely cut off from the outside world. I've withdrawn due to tragedy. I've hit rock bottom."
Ron continued to lie face down on the floor, muttering depressing nonsense. Toto felt bad, so he asked if Ron had any hobbies to cheer him up. He handed him a photo... of himself knitting a 100-meter scarf?!

Next, Ron revealed that he had named every hair strand on his arms and created a diagram.
"I'm a piece of junk frozen in time. You should just leave. Say hi to Kiku-san for me."

"I refuse. You're not the first one. I guess Kiku-san really wants me to make a comeback, though he knows better than anyone why I stopped."
Stopped...? But Kiku said I should ask him for help!
Toto insisted, but Ron kept shrugging him off, as if not caring about the deaths happening.

"Good idea. You're better off praying for help at the shrine."

"It's simple. The only place you'd get that pine pollen on your shoulder would be the path leading to Wakana Shrine. Judging by the unopened canned coffee in your pocket, and the sound of the coins in the other pocket, I can tell you bought it to get change for an offering."

"Your habit of saying whatever you think without hesitation is indicative of a spoiled child. And the fact you didn't walk down the center of the shrine path suggests you were brought up in a refined environment. It seems you were raised by your grandparents."

"That naive personality is a hindrance for someone in the investigative department. Did you come for help so you don't end up getting transferred?"
